LION
The lion, scientifically known as Panthera leo, is one of the largest and most iconic carnivores, belonging to the family Felidae and the genus Panthera. Lions are primarily found in sub-Saharan Africa, with a small population also present in the Gir Forest of India.

GAZELLE
Gazelles are a genus of antelope that belong to the family Bovidae, which includes other familiar animals such as cattle, goats, and sheep. Gazelles are native to Africa and Asia, inhabiting various types of habitats including grasslands, savannas, and deserts.

ELEPHANT
The elephant is the largest land animal on Earth, belonging to the family Elephantidae and the order Proboscidea. There are three species of elephants: the African bush elephant, the African forest elephant, and the Asian elephant.

LEOPARD
The leopard, scientifically known as Panthera pardus, is a large and agile carnivore belonging to the Felidae family and Panthera genus. Leopards are native to various habitats across Africa and Asia, ranging from dense forests to savannas and even urban areas.
